# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primarily **comedians and performers** working the London circuit — people hunting for an open mic, showcase, or pro slot to perform at. Their context is practical and often on-the-go: they're on a phone deciding where to gig this week, and they care about the details that determine whether a night is worth it — night type, level (new / experienced / pro), bringer rules, area, and nearest station.

A secondary audience of **fans/audience** uses the same listings to find a night out, but the product optimizes its defaults for performers, since that's what the core data describes.

## Product Purpose

FindComedy is a directory of live comedy nights across London, kept fresh by the people who actually go. It exists so a performer can find the right night in seconds instead of trawling Facebook groups and WhatsApp threads. Success is **speed-to-decision**: a user lands, scans, and knows where they're gigging — with trustworthy, current information.

## Brand Personality

**Gritty, honest, local.** This is the real London comedy circuit — a bit rough around the edges, insider, made by people who do the rooms, not a polished corporate product. Voice is plain-spoken and direct, never salesy or cute. It should feel like a knowledgeable friend telling you where the good rooms are, not a brand talking at you.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / startup aesthetic.** No purple gradients, hero-metric blocks, identical icon-card grids, or glassmorphism. If it looks like an AI generated it, it has failed.
- **Corporate ticketing sites** (Eventbrite / Ticketmaster). No dense, ad-cluttered, soulless listing walls.
- **Over-designed or slow.** Nothing that gets between the user and the listings — no heavy animation, no giant hero images, no spectacle for its own sake. The data is the point.

(Note: the "loud neon comedy-club cliché" — brick walls, spotlights, garish neon "LOL", cheesy stand-up stock photos — is also off the table by default; gritty-local is carried through type, color, and copy, not props.)

## Design Principles

1. **The listing is the product.** Every design decision is judged by whether it helps a performer decide faster. Decoration that doesn't serve scanning gets cut.
2. **Honest over slick.** Real, current information presented plainly beats a polished veneer. Show the bringer rule and the level up front; don't hide the unglamorous details that performers actually need.
3. **Made by locals, for locals.** Insider framing and London-specific cues (areas, nearest stations) over generic geography. It should feel like circuit knowledge, not a database.
4. **Fast on a phone, in the wild.** Mobile-first, legible in bad light, quick to load. The user is standing on a platform deciding their week.
5. **Earn every flourish.** Personality comes from voice, typography, and a confident palette — not from effects. One deliberate moment beats ten reflexive ones.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG AA** as the baseline: AA contrast on body and large text (including in dark mode), full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, and adequate mobile tap targets. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any motion added. Solid, dependable defaults — not a specialized AAA target, but never an afterthought.
